Patterns of Firearm-Related Violence
By Jessica Lerner
Dec. 11, 2020
While over 39,000 individuals died from gunshots across the country in 2018, research examining the effectiveness of firearm-related laws been contradictory or inconclusive.

And while research is limited, the answer appears to be, in part, that states with stricter gun control laws have fewer firearm deaths — sometimes dramatically fewer — than those that don't.

"A higher count of a state's firearm laws is associated with fewer total firearm deaths," said Ye Liu, a doctoral student in the Department of Health Care Organization and Policy at the University of Alabama at Birmingham.

For example, in 2018, Mississippi, Alabama, Wyoming, Missouri, and Louisiana had the highest firearm-related death rates. All of these states, though, 20 or fewer firearm-related laws.

In contrast, New Jersey, Hawaii, New York, Massachusetts, and Rhode Island had the lowest firearm-related death rates. Each of these states had 50 or more gun-related laws.
